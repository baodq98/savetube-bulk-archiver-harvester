## Getting Started with Your First Archive

1. **Identify your source** – a public playlist, a user channel, or a curated list of video URLs.
2. **Configure your preferences** – output quality, naming convention (e.g., `{channel}-{title}-{date}`), destination folder.
3. **Launch the pipeline** – the Kit begins scanning, negotiating, and saving. You can step away confidently.
4. **Review and organize** – after completion, your media is filed locally, ready for viewing, editing, or long-term preservation.

> **Pro Tip:** Use the **“Dry Run” mode** first for large collections. The Kit will simulate the entire download flow and show you what will be saved, what might be missing, and any quality issues—without touching a single file.

## Architecture & Design Philosophy

The Savetube Stream Kit is modular by nature, built like a set of interlocking gears. Each component handles a specific task, and they communicate through light, predictable protocols. This design means you can **replace or upgrade individual pieces** without disturbing the others.

### Core Components

- **Stream Negotiator** – Handles connection with source platforms, negotiates available quality levels, and manages rate limiting respectfully.
- **Batch Scheduler** – Orchestrates multiple save operations, respecting system resources and network bandwidth.
- **Metadata Enricher** – Attaches description, upload date, duration, and embedded captions to each saved file.
- **Integrity Verifier** – After saving, runs a checksum and partial playback analysis to confirm the file is complete and playable.

### Why Not Just Use a Browser Extension?

Browser extensions come and go. They update, break, and sometimes disappear entirely. The Savetube Stream Kit is a **standalone, versioned toolkit** that you control. When platforms change their stream protocols, we update the Kit—but your pipeline remains yours. No third-party dependency, no centralized server, no data leaving your machine.
